                Electron concentration                                  Hole concentration

When the gate voltage is zero, the area near the gate has few electrons and cannot be able to conduct the current, so the electron and hole concentration are both very low on the middle part.

When the gate voltage is 4V, electrons are attracted to gather near the gate, the source and drain can be connected. Therefore, the electron concentration in the upper part is high.

The value of concentration of electron and hole are just opposite to each other in the same position.

Electric potential

When the gate voltage is zero, of course electrons cannot move form source to drain because the electrons cannot gather on the
upper side of the mono-silicon. The diffusion between source and
drain is not possible to happen.

When the voltage is 4V, the electrons are attracted to aggregate in the gate area, which could create a conductive path as shown in the graph.

This is the diagram which shows the relationship of terminal current and gate voltage.

When the gate voltage is around 1.1V, the current starts to exist. (1.1V is the value of the band gap) Only if the voltage exceed this value, the electrons can move between the bands so that the current occurs.

Conclusion

MOSFET have advantages over other types of transistors for its greater integration density and simpler geometry. MOS transistors consist of source and drain diffusions, with a gate that controls whether the transistor is on. By applying voltage on the gate, we can help the electrons aggregate between source and drain, which enables current flows through them. For given gate voltage, increasing voltage differences between source and drain increases current from source to drain. From the modeling process, I find out that the area close to the gate has high electron concentration and low hole concentration when the voltage is 4V. Instead, when the gate voltage is 0V, this area has low electron concentration and high hole concentration, which means the electric potential in this area is lower than that with gate voltage of 4V. Moreover, from further exploration, we can see that when gate voltage is around 1.1, which is the value of the band gap, there is current in the transistor.
